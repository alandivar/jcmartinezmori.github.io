About

I am a first year PhD student in Geomechanics at Northwestern University. My advisor is Alessandro Rotta Loria. My research interests lie in the intersection of geomechanics, engineering mechanics and energy, in a context of engineered materials, specifically electrokinetic remediation of soil materials to improve their properties and increase resilience of structures.

I received a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in May 2017 and a Master in Science from Stanford University in March 2019. While at Illinois, I was advised by Dr. Timothy Stark and my research involved exploring the Fully Softened Shear Strength for Slope Stability Analysis. In Stanford, I was advised by Dr. Ronaldo Borja and my research involved using Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) to model flow-slides of natural slopes. Additionally, I have worked as a geotechnical engineering intern in Cal Engineering & Geology in San Jose (supervised by Dan Peluso). My worked included performing 3D settlement and slope stability analyses and performing geotechnical field investigations and posterior geotechnical reports.
